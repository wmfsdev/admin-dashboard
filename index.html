Your Ramens!

Announcements

Shio

One of the two most popular ways to serve clear assari broth, and it's the way to go if you want to taste the soup itself. Shio means salt in Japanese, so shio ramen is seasoned simply with salt.

Tonkotsu

It is said to be invented by mistake: A ramen maker in Fukuoka (a prefecture on Japan's Kyushu Island) accidentally let his pork bones boil for too long. It made the broth cloudy and fatty but full of rich, porky flavor. His customers loved it, and the rest is history!

Shoyu

A clear ramen broth that is similar to salt-based shio, but it's bolder and more heavily seasoned thanks to the addition of soy sauce. Shoyu is unique because it refers to a Japanese style where soybeans are fermented with wheat that dates back to the 13th century.

Miso

Another popular ramen seasoning is miso, a fermented soybean paste that adds sweet, salty, savory, and funky vibes to any dish. There are three types of miso: white, yellow, and red.

Yuzu

Not to be confused with the spicy yuzu kosho, yuzu ramen is light and delicate thanks to the addition of yuzu citrus juice. Yuzu is one of the main ingredients in ponzu sauce, and it's sour and tart but bright and refreshing.

Spicy

There's no singular way to make ramen spicy, but these bowls all have one thing in common: They bring the heat!

Yasai

Vegetarian ramen-goers love yasai ramen, which is topped with stir-fried vegetables. The type of vegetables added depends on the chef, but bowls can include mushrooms, eggplant, carrots, bok choy, peppers, onions, bean sprouts, and more.

Tsukemen

A specialized type of ramen, and it's not offered at all ramen joints. Instead of serving the noodles in the broth, the two are separated and the noodles are cold. The broth here is condensed and concentrated to make it extra flavorful.

Tantanmen

If you love spicy Szechuan noodles, you should absolutely try tantanmen. This Japanese version turns the Chinese favorite dan dan mein into a brothy bowl, swapping out the original's thick sesame paste sauce for a broth infused with similar seasonings.
